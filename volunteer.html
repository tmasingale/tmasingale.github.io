Volunteer to be a coach

Volunteering as a coach for the Spokane Mayor’s Cup event is easy!

Volunteering as a coach for the Spokane Mayor’s Cup event is easy. The obligation is for the day of the event only. The primary duty of coaches at our event is to act as a helper and a role model for teams. The coaches are given a guide to help them to help participants. You will be assigned one or more teams. The true benefit of having industry representatives as coaches is to introduce participants to people do cybersecurity related things as part of their professional lives. The technical challenges may not be areas in which you may not be familiar. We have some guides and clues that you can share with participants. You can’t help them directly, but you can give them advice or just chat with them about what you do at work. This is a learning event. Help us to introduce college and high school students to the professional world of cybersecurity. The event is Saturday February 12, 2022. It runs from 8:30 to 5:30. You do not need to attend the whole day. Lunch and refreshments will be provided. To volunteer email ssteiner@ewu.edu

WHEN: Saturday, February 5th, 2022.

WHERE: The Eastern Washington University
Catalyst Building
601 E. Riverside
Spokane, WA 99203

Registered teams must check-in between 8:00 and 8:30 the morning of the event for room assignments.

Check-in will be held in the Catalyst Building lobby.

We are a five minute walk from the WSU Riverpoint campus and a ten minute walk from the Convention Center

For questions about the event or registration, contact the event organizer, Stu Steiner – ssteiner@ewu.edu
